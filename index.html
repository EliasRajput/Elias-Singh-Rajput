During the quarantine, I had the opportunity to self-reflect and see that I was weak in planning my course work. Learning at home without the direct support and guidance of my teachers, counselors, and mentors, I saw that the responsibility would be solely on me to improve on my weaknesses. Thus, I began to work on strategic planning to help make my academic success path efficient and stress-free. In April 2020, I became familiar with the principles of strategic planning. I realized that in order to prepare in advance for the upcoming school year, I had to say no to distractions, make a list of resources, and use a two-by-two matrix to filter and evaluate all materials I might require or not. A two-by-two matrix is a tool that I use to help prioritize supplemental and academic resources. The way this works is, I look at two objective functions of my choice, plot them against one another, and then place the potential resources on that grid. To improve my academics approach, I first created a graph with "study time" on the x-axis and "efficient learning" on the y-axis. After that, I drew out four quadrants within the graph. I placed possible resource initiatives into the quadrants. In the first quadrant, top left, I organized the most useful resources that required minimal study time but offered effective learning, such as Anki spaced repetition flashcards and the Memory Palace technique. In the second quadrant, bottom left, I wrote the resources that required less study time but were not relatively efficient, such as Bozeman science, Khan Academy, and visual and audio learning sources in general. In the third quadrant, top right, I wrote the efficient resources, but that required a lot of study time, such as a scientific multi-phase experiment. In the fourth quadrant, bottom right, I wrote the most inefficient resources. These resources required a lot of studying time and were not practical such as reference textbooks.
